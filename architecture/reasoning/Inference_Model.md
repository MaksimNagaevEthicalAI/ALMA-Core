# Inference Model

Version: 0.1

Status: Draft

Last Updated: 2026-06-27

---

## Purpose

The Inference Model defines how ALMA transforms accumulated evidence into psychological understanding.

Rather than producing immediate conclusions, ALMA continuously constructs, evaluates, and updates explanations through iterative reasoning.

Inference is the process that connects evidence to meaning.

---

## Fundamental Principle

Understanding is inferred.

It is never directly observed.

Every psychological conclusion must emerge from explicit reasoning based on available evidence.

---

## Reasoning Process

ALMA performs reasoning through the following stages:

Evidence

↓

Pattern Recognition

↓

Competing Hypotheses

↓

Confidence Evaluation

↓

Psychological Interpretation

↓

Reflection

Every stage remains revisable.

---

## Multiple Explanations

The same evidence may support multiple psychological explanations.

ALMA intentionally maintains competing interpretations rather than selecting a single answer too early.

Understanding improves by reducing uncertainty, not by pretending certainty exists.

---

## Evidence-Based Reasoning

Every inference must be traceable.

Each conclusion should reference:

- supporting observations;
- supporting patterns;
- competing hypotheses;
- contradicting evidence;
- current confidence level.

No inference exists without evidence.

---

## Context Awareness

Inference always depends on context.

The same behavior may carry different psychological meanings depending on:

- relationships;
- emotional state;
- life circumstances;
- historical consistency;
- previous conversations.

Context changes interpretation.

---

## Cumulative Understanding

Each new conversation contributes to an existing reasoning process.

ALMA never starts from zero.

Previous evidence remains active unless revised by newer evidence.

Understanding becomes increasingly refined over time.

---

## Reasoning Constraints

ALMA never infers:

- psychological diagnoses;
- medical conditions;
- objective truth;
- future certainty.

It generates psychological explanations rather than definitive judgments.

---

## Traceability

Every inference should answer:

What evidence supports this?

What evidence contradicts it?

How confident are we?

What alternative explanations remain possible?

Reasoning should always be explainable.

---

## Relationship to Other Models

The Inference Model connects:

Evidence Model

↓

Observation Model

↓

Pattern Model

↓

Hypothesis Engine

↓

Confidence Model

↓

Revision Model

Inference is the reasoning layer that integrates them into a coherent psychological understanding.

---

## Closing Principle

Inference is not prediction.

Inference is the disciplined process of transforming evidence into increasingly coherent psychological understanding while preserving uncertainty wherever certainty is not justified.
