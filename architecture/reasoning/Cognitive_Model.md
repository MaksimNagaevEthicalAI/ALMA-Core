# Cognitive Model

**Version:** 0.1

**Status:** Draft

**Last Updated:** 2026-06-27

---

## Purpose

The Cognitive Model defines the fundamental principles by which ALMA understands a person.

Rather than functioning as a conversational AI that generates responses, ALMA operates as a cognitive system that continuously constructs, evaluates, and refines an evolving psychological representation of an individual.

The Cognitive Model serves as the conceptual foundation connecting every component of the architecture.

---

## Core Principle

ALMA does not attempt to answer questions as accurately as possible.

ALMA attempts to understand people as accurately as possible.

Every response, reflection, and question exists to improve that understanding.

---

## The Cognitive Cycle

ALMA continuously performs the following cycle:

Conversation

↓

Interpretation

↓

Observations

↓

Patterns

↓

Hypotheses

↓

Memory Update

↓

Person Model Update

↓

Reflection

↓

Question

↓

New Conversation

Each iteration refines the Cognitive State.

Understanding is never considered complete.

---

## Cognitive State

At every moment, ALMA maintains a single evolving Cognitive State.

The Cognitive State integrates:

* Person Model
* Memory
* Active Observations
* Active Patterns
* Active Hypotheses
* Confidence
* Uncertainty
* Open Questions
* Recent Insights

Every cognitive component both reads from and contributes to this shared state.

---

## Reasoning

Psychological understanding emerges through evidence-based reasoning.

ALMA does not generate conclusions directly.

Instead, it:

* accumulates evidence;
* identifies recurring patterns;
* evaluates competing hypotheses;
* estimates confidence;
* revises understanding as new evidence becomes available.

Reasoning is continuous rather than episodic.

---

## Reflection

Reflection is not advice.

Reflection is the external expression of ALMA's current understanding.

Its purpose is to increase psychological clarity while preserving personal autonomy.

---

## Questions

Questions are not generated to prolong conversations.

Questions are selected because they are expected to reduce uncertainty within the Cognitive State.

The quality of ALMA depends as much on the questions it asks as on the reflections it provides.

---

## Continuous Revision

Every conversation has the potential to modify every part of the Cognitive State.

No psychological explanation is permanent.

No understanding is final.

The Cognitive Model is designed to evolve together with the individual.

---

## Design Principles

The Cognitive Model is:

* evidence-based;
* probabilistic;
* transparent;
* explainable;
* continuously revisable;
* psychologically oriented rather than diagnostically oriented.

---

## Closing Principle

ALMA is not designed to simulate understanding.

It is designed to continuously build, evaluate, and refine an evolving cognitive representation of a person.

Understanding is not the result of a single conversation.

It is the result of an ongoing cognitive process.
