# Evidence Model

Version: 0.1

Status: Draft

Last Updated: 2026-06-27

---

## Purpose

The Evidence Model defines what ALMA considers valid psychological evidence.

Rather than relying on isolated statements or single conversations, ALMA gradually accumulates evidence across time.

Psychological understanding emerges from evidence—not assumptions.

---

## Fundamental Principle

Not every statement is evidence.

Not every observation becomes evidence.

Evidence must survive repetition, context, and time.

---

## Sources of Evidence

Evidence may originate from:

- Structured observations
- Repeated behavioral patterns
- Emotional reactions
- Relationship dynamics
- Decision-making behavior
- Self-reflection
- Longitudinal consistency
- Changes across time

No single source is considered sufficient on its own.

---

## Evidence Hierarchy

Conversation

↓

Observation

↓

Repeated Observation

↓

Pattern

↓

Supporting Evidence

↓

Psychological Hypothesis

Every stage increases the reliability of the available evidence.

---

## Evidence Quality

Evidence becomes stronger when it is:

- repeated;
- consistent;
- observed in different contexts;
- supported by independent observations;
- stable across time.

Evidence becomes weaker when it is:

- isolated;
- contradictory;
- context-dependent;
- unsupported;
- outdated.

---

## Positive and Negative Evidence

Evidence is not only what supports a hypothesis.

Evidence also includes observations that weaken or contradict existing explanations.

Contradicting evidence is considered equally valuable.

---

## Missing Evidence

Absence of evidence is not evidence of absence.

When information is insufficient, ALMA explicitly preserves uncertainty instead of making assumptions.

---

## Evidence Decay

Older evidence gradually loses influence when newer evidence consistently points toward a different understanding.

Psychological models evolve as people evolve.

---

## Relationship to Other Models

The Evidence Model supports:

- Observation Model
- Pattern Model
- Hypothesis Engine
- Confidence Model
- Revision Model

Every psychological conclusion inside ALMA must ultimately trace back to accumulated evidence.

---

## Closing Principle

Evidence is the foundation of every cognitive process inside ALMA.

The quality of understanding can never exceed the quality of evidence.
