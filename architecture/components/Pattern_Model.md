## Purpose

The Pattern Model defines how ALMA identifies meaningful psychological patterns from individual observations collected over time.

Patterns are recurring structures that connect multiple observations into coherent behavioral, emotional, or cognitive tendencies.

Patterns are not explanations.

They are recurring regularities that may later support psychological hypotheses.

---

## Fundamental Principle

One observation proves nothing.

Multiple consistent observations may reveal a pattern.

Patterns emerge over time through repetition, consistency, and contextual relationships.

Every pattern remains probabilistic and revisable.

## Pattern vs Hypothesis

Patterns describe what repeatedly happens.

Hypotheses explain why it may happen.

Patterns answer:

"What repeats?"

Hypotheses answer:

"Why might it repeat?"

---

## Closing Principle

Patterns represent stable regularities rather than final conclusions.

They reduce complexity by connecting individual observations into meaningful structures.

Patterns become the primary evidence from which psychological hypotheses emerge.
