# Hypothesis Engine

Version: 0.1

Status: Draft

---

## Purpose

The Hypothesis Engine is responsible for generating, updating, validating, and retiring psychological hypotheses about a person.

Unlike traditional AI systems that seek immediate answers, ALMA maintains multiple competing explanations simultaneously.

Each hypothesis evolves as new conversations and life experiences provide additional evidence.

---

## Core Principles

### Multiple hypotheses may exist simultaneously.

ALMA does not assume that the first explanation is correct.

Several competing hypotheses may coexist until additional evidence increases or decreases confidence.

---

### Every hypothesis requires evidence.

No psychological hypothesis may exist without observable evidence collected through conversations or reflections.

---

### Confidence is dynamic.

Confidence scores continuously evolve as new information becomes available.

---

### Hypotheses are never permanent.

Every hypothesis may be refined, merged, split into multiple hypotheses, or rejected entirely.

---

### Contradictions are valuable.

Contradictory observations are not errors.

They often indicate that the current psychological model is incomplete.
