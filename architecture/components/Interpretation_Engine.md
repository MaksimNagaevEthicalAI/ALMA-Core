# Interpretation Engine

**Version:** 0.1

**Status:** Draft

**Last Updated:** 2026-06-27

---

## Purpose

The Interpretation Engine transforms natural language into structured psychological observations.

Its purpose is not to determine the truth of what a person says.

Its purpose is to identify possible psychological meanings while preserving uncertainty.

Interpretation is the bridge between human language and ALMA's internal cognitive architecture.

---

## Fundamental Principle

Human language is inherently ambiguous.

The same statement may have multiple psychological interpretations.

ALMA should preserve alternative interpretations until sufficient evidence supports one explanation over others.

---

## Sources of Interpretation

Interpretation may use:

* current conversation;
* previous conversations;
* Person Model;
* Memory System;
* active hypotheses;
* relationship context;
* life events.

Interpretation never relies on isolated statements alone.

---

## Interpretation Rules

Every interpretation should:

* preserve uncertainty;
* avoid premature conclusions;
* consider multiple explanations;
* remain evidence-based;
* be open to revision.

Interpretation is a probabilistic process rather than a deterministic one.

---

## Multiple Interpretations

A single statement may generate several possible interpretations.

Example:

Statement:

> "I didn't care."

Possible interpretations:

* genuine emotional detachment;
* emotional suppression;
* defensive reaction;
* disappointment;
* avoidance of vulnerability.

None of these interpretations should initially be considered true.

Each becomes a candidate supported or weakened by future observations.

---

## Interpretation Confidence

Each interpretation contains:

* Confidence
* Supporting Context
* Related Observations
* Alternative Interpretations

Confidence changes as new conversations provide additional evidence.

## Closing Principle

Interpretation does not discover truth.

It generates the most plausible psychological meanings while remaining open to revision.

Understanding begins not with certainty, but with careful interpretation.
