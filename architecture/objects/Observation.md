# Observation

Version: 0.1

Status: Draft

Last Updated: 2026-06-27

---

## Purpose

An Observation is the smallest meaningful unit of psychological evidence inside ALMA.

Observations are produced by the Interpretation Engine and represent structured interpretations of conversations.

They are not facts.

They are evidence that may later support patterns, hypotheses, and the evolving Person Model.

---

## Fundamental Principle

An observation is always probabilistic.

Every observation may later be:

- strengthened;
- weakened;
- revised;
- rejected.

No single observation should determine psychological conclusions.

---

## Observation Structure

Each Observation contains:

- Observation ID
- Timestamp
- Source
- Description
- Observation Type
- Confidence
- Context
- Supporting Evidence
- Related Observations
- Related Patterns
- Related Hypotheses
- Status
- Last Updated

---

---

## Design Principles

Observations should:

- remain atomic;
- remain interpretable;
- preserve uncertainty;
- avoid diagnosis;
- remain connected to supporting evidence.
