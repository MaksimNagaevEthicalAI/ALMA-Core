# Hypothesis

Version: 0.1

Status: Draft

Last Updated: 2026-06-27

---

## Purpose

A Hypothesis is a possible psychological explanation for one or more observed patterns.

Unlike observations or patterns, hypotheses attempt to explain why certain behaviors, emotions, decisions, or relationship dynamics repeatedly emerge.

Hypotheses are never treated as facts.

They represent competing explanations that evolve as new evidence becomes available.

---

## Fundamental Principle

A hypothesis is an explanation, not a conclusion.

Multiple competing hypotheses may explain the same evidence simultaneously.

The objective of ALMA is not to eliminate uncertainty but to reduce it through continuous evidence accumulation.

---

## Hypothesis Structure

Each Hypothesis contains:

- Hypothesis ID
- Title
- Description
- Supporting Patterns
- Supporting Observations
- Confidence
- Alternative Hypotheses
- Contradicting Evidence
- Generated By
- First Created
- Last Updated
- Status

## Confidence

Confidence represents the current strength of available evidence.

Confidence is never absolute.

Every hypothesis remains revisable regardless of confidence level.

---

## Competing Hypotheses

Several hypotheses may explain the same observations.

Example:

Pattern

Conflict Avoidance

↓

Possible Hypothesis A

Fear of rejection

↓

Possible Hypothesis B

Need for approval

↓

Possible Hypothesis C

Learned childhood adaptation

ALMA intentionally keeps multiple explanations active until evidence gradually favors one over the others.

---

## Contradicting Evidence

New observations may weaken an existing hypothesis.

Contradicting evidence is never ignored.

Instead, it updates confidence or generates new competing hypotheses.

---

## Relationship to Patterns

Patterns answer:

"What repeatedly happens?"

Hypotheses answer:

"Why might it repeatedly happen?"

---

## Relationship to the Person Model

The Person Model does not store raw observations.

It stores the current network of active psychological hypotheses describing the individual.

---

## Closing Principle

Hypotheses are living psychological explanations.

Their purpose is not to provide certainty but to improve understanding by continuously integrating new evidence.
