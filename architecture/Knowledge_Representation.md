# Purpose

The Knowledge Representation defines how psychological knowledge exists inside ALMA.

Knowledge is not stored as isolated facts or static profiles.

Instead, ALMA continuously builds, refines, connects, and revises multiple levels of psychological understanding.

The goal is not to accumulate information, but to maintain an evolving cognitive representation of a person.

---

Psychological understanding develops progressively.

Each level increases abstraction while preserving the evidence from previous levels.

---

# Levels of Knowledge

ALMA represents knowledge across multiple interconnected levels.

## Raw Conversation

Original user communication.

No psychological interpretation.

---

## Interpretations

Possible meanings extracted from the conversation.

Interpretations are tentative and may later be revised.

---

## Observations

Structured evidence describing specific psychological signals.

Observations remain descriptive rather than explanatory.

---

## Patterns

Repeated regularities discovered across multiple observations.

Patterns identify recurring tendencies.

---

## Hypotheses

Evidence-based psychological explanations.

Hypotheses explain why patterns may exist.

They always remain probabilistic.

---

## Long-term Memory

Confirmed psychological knowledge accumulated over time.

Memory preserves stable understanding while remaining revisable.

---

## Person Model

The current integrated psychological representation of the individual.

The Person Model continuously evolves as new evidence becomes available.

---

## Cognitive State

The complete psychological understanding maintained during reasoning.

The Cognitive State integrates every active level of knowledge.

---

ALMA represents multiple complementary forms of knowledge.

* Observational knowledge

* Cognitive knowledge
* Relational knowledge

* Meta knowledge

Together these provide a multidimensional psychological representation.

---

# Confidence

Every knowledge element contains an associated confidence level.

Confidence increases when:

* evidence accumulates;
* observations repeat;
* multiple sources support the same conclusion.

Confidence decreases when:

* contradictory evidence appears;
* uncertainty increases;
* alternative explanations become plausible.

Confidence is continuously updated rather than fixed.

---

# Contradictions

Contradictions are preserved rather than discarded.

When new evidence conflicts with previous understanding:

Previous Knowledge

↓

Contradictory Evidence

↓

Competing Interpretation

↓

Confidence Revision

↓

Updated Cognitive State

Conflicting evidence contributes to deeper understanding instead of replacing previous knowledge immediately.

---

# Knowledge Revision

No psychological knowledge is considered permanent.

Every observation, pattern, and hypothesis may be strengthened, weakened, refined, or replaced.

Revision is an essential characteristic of understanding rather than an indication of error.

---

# Relationship to Cognitive State

Knowledge Representation provides the internal structure of the Cognitive State.

The Cognitive State integrates:

* Person Model
* Memory
* Active Observations
* Active Patterns
* Active Hypotheses
* Confidence
* Uncertainty
* Open Questions
* Recent Insights

Every cognitive component both consumes and refines this shared representation.

---

# Design Principles

Knowledge inside ALMA is:

* evidence-based;
* hierarchical;
* probabilistic;
* explainable;
* continuously revisable;
* psychologically meaningful;
* cumulative.

Understanding improves through refinement rather than replacement.

---

# Closing Principle

Knowledge inside ALMA is not a collection of facts.

It is a continuously evolving psychological representation built from evidence, refined through reasoning, and revised as new understanding emerges.
